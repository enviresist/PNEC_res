Mission and funding

Antibiotics are drugs that either kill bacteria or inhibit their proliferation. However, even sub-lethal or sub-inhibitory concentrations of antibiotics select for drug-resistant bacterial phenotypes, aggravating the global crisis of antimicrobial resistance. The critical concentration above which selection for resistant phenotypes occurs is known as the minimum selective concentration (MSC). Lower-bound estimates of the MSC that account for variabilities within and across bacterial genera can serve as predicted no-effect concentrations regarding resistance selection (PNECR).

This web page provides PNECR estimates for a large suite of antibiotics currently used in human medicin. The presented material represents the outcome of a research project run by GWT / the TU Dresden Institute of Hydrobiology with funding from the German Environment Agency.
